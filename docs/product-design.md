# Product Design

## Product Positioning

AnytimeSpeak is an AI English speaking practice tool for learners who want realistic, scenario-based conversation training. It focuses on practical speaking confidence, useful corrections, and measurable improvement.

The MVP is designed for a course demo: it should be stable, easy to understand, and reproducible without private credentials.

## User Flow

1. Open the app.
2. Choose a practice scenario.
3. Read the scenario goal and AI role.
4. Start the conversation.
5. Type or speak English input.
6. Receive an AI role-play reply.
7. Receive lightweight feedback during practice when appropriate.
8. End the session.
9. Review summary, corrections, suggestions, and scores.

## MVP Scenarios

### Interview

- AI role: Hiring manager.
- User role: Job candidate.
- Practice goal: Answer interview questions clearly, explain experience, and ask professional follow-up questions.
- Opening line: "Hi, thanks for joining today. Could you briefly introduce yourself and tell me why you are interested in this role?"

### Restaurant Ordering

- AI role: Restaurant server.
- User role: Customer.
- Practice goal: Order food, ask about recommendations, clarify preferences, and handle simple service questions.
- Opening line: "Welcome! Are you ready to order, or would you like a few recommendations first?"

### Meeting

- AI role: Team lead.
- User role: Team member.
- Practice goal: Share progress, discuss blockers, ask for clarification, and agree on next steps.
- Opening line: "Let's start with your update. What progress have you made since our last meeting?"

## Feedback Design

### Per-Turn Feedback

Per-turn feedback should be brief and non-disruptive. The system should avoid correcting every small issue during active conversation.

Suggested per-turn feedback includes:

- One grammar correction when there is a clear mistake.
- One expression improvement when the user's sentence sounds unnatural.
- A short encouragement or confirmation when the message is already clear.

Per-turn feedback can be shown in a separate feedback area instead of interrupting the role-play reply.

### Post-Session Summary

The post-session summary should include:

- Overall performance summary.
- Key strengths.
- Repeated grammar issues.
- Better expressions the user can reuse.
- Scenario goal completion.
- Suggested next practice focus.
- Quantitative scores.

## Scoring Design

Scores use a 0 to 100 scale. MVP scoring can be generated by AI or mock rules, then refined later.

### Grammar Score

Measures sentence correctness, tense usage, word order, and basic grammar accuracy.

### Expression Score

Measures naturalness, vocabulary choice, politeness, and suitability for the scenario.

### Fluency Score

Measures smoothness of communication. In text-only mode, this can be approximated by message completeness and response length. In speech mode, it can later include pauses, restarts, and recognition confidence.

### Scenario Completion Score

Measures whether the user achieved the scenario goal, responded appropriately, and handled key conversation steps.

### Overall Score

Combines grammar, expression, fluency, and scenario completion into one summary score.

Initial weighting:

- Grammar: 25%
- Expression: 25%
- Fluency: 20%
- Scenario completion: 30%

## Voice Interaction Design

### Speech Input

- Use browser `SpeechRecognition` when available.
- Let users start and stop recording manually.
- Convert recognized speech into editable text before sending.
- Show clear fallback behavior when speech recognition is unavailable.

### Speech Playback

- Use browser `SpeechSynthesis` for AI replies.
- Provide play and stop controls.
- Consider optional auto-play after each AI reply.

### Text Fallback

Text input is always available. It is the required MVP baseline and ensures the demo works even when speech APIs are unavailable or unstable.
